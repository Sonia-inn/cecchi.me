America's New Internet Economy
After watching the US government threaten the Internet with bills like SOPA and PIPA, internet entreprenuers from Reddit set out to

"Campaign for the internet"

Traveling from Denver, CO, to Danville, KY, during the presidential & vice presidential debates, the Internet 2012 Bus Tour explored the potential that the Open Internet holds to promote our economy - not just in Silicon Valley, but in cities and towns (and farms!) all over America. SIlicon Prairie: America's New Internet Economy brings high-tech examples of success, good news, and growth from business owners, startups, politicians, and Internet users in The Heartland.
Denver
Danville
Following the showing, filmmakers and Internet entrepreneurs will discuss why government and citizens alike should care about preserving (and developing) the Internet Economy in every city and every town in America. How does Internet policy affect individuals, startups, and the spirit of innovation in The Heartland of America? Why do successful startups continue to choose Omaha or Des Moines rather than a startup hub, like San Francisco? And why have a number of fledgling startups moved from Boston and New York City to Kansas City to bootstrap their businesses?
